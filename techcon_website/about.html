History

ShadowTech began as a late-night meetup of security researchers and curious builders in 2018. What started as a handful of talks in small hacker spaces quickly grew into an annual two-day conference that blends hands-on workshops, technical deep-dives, and experimental demos. Over the years ShadowTech has evolved from an underground meetup into a respected niche event focused on advanced systems, privacy, and adversarial thinking.

Mission

ShadowTech’s mission is to provide a safe, practical space where curious engineers, researchers, and creators can explore the darker edges of technology responsibly — from defensive security and privacy engineering to experimentation with novel system designs. The conference emphasizes practical learning, ethical disclosure, and community-driven knowledge sharing.

Past Speakers

Dr. Mira Kade

Dr. Kade is known for her practical research in intrusion detection systems and adversarial testing. At ShadowTech she led workshops demystifying real-world attack chains and defensive mitigations for infrastructure teams.

Jalen Park

Jalen focuses on resilient distributed systems and tooling for observability. His ShadowTech talks introduced practical chaos-testing recipes and lightweight observability stacks for small teams.

Aisha Conte

Aisha’s work bridges privacy-preserving design with developer tooling. Her presentations focus on practical privacy patterns and how to bake privacy into team workflows without slowing development.
